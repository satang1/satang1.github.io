The following reflection is a response to a journal response for my Summer 2019 Experiential Learning course: 

	1. Complete a short quiz on Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs. Then, prepare to discuss your findings.

	2.  Write a 150- to 250-word post that addresses all of these questions:

	Recall:
	Recall a situation where you or someone close to you sacrificed one need over another. Be specific as to which of Maslow’s needs were at issue. 

	Reflect:
	Is Maslow's theory a useful way to better understand your needs and the needs of others? Why or why not?

<b>Note:</b> This post assumes familiarity with Bolman & Deal's <i> Reframing Organizations</i> textbook. 


Perhaps a common situation in which Maslow's theory can be applied is weight loss. I had a friend who was rather active on social media, and whenever she posted pictures on Instagram of FB, she would be self-conscious about how she looked in comparison with her friends. As a result, she felt pressured to look and be a certain size. In the process of losing weight, she sacrificed her physiological needs (i.e. ate less, cut out foods, etc.), because her need to belong and be loved (by others and herself) took precedence. I would also add that her need for self-esteem was also a factor, because looking a certain way (in photos and/or videos) helps to gain "likes", attention, and recognition. 

After taking this quiz, I realized that it can be quite difficult to determine which of Maslow's needs were taking precedence or which were being sacrificed, and this is mainly because I think that there can be several needs or sacrifices taking place. As a result, I think that when it comes to using Maslow's theory to understand personal needs or the needs of others, it is important to consider that there may be more than one level/component in the hierarchy of needs involved in the specific circumstance. For instance, if we take a look at the situation where a women was diagnosed with diabetes and is cutting out certain foods. Her bitter interaction with family and friends could be sacrificing her need to belong and be loved (the "correct" answer), but I also think that she is sacrificing some of her physiological needs since her body might be expecting certain foods for regular functions. But overall, I do think that Maslow's theory is a useful way to better understand human needs because each "level" of need is distinct and general enough to encompass the key motivations and desires propelling human behavior, decision, and attitude. Additionally, the hierarchical levels make sense since the modern world satisfies majority of humans' base needs, and as a result, people begin to look for more (internal motivations) in life.
